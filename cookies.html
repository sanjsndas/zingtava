Our Cookies Policy

Understanding how Zingtava uses cookies and similar technologies to enhance your culinary journey.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, as well as to provide information to the owners of the site. Cookies enable a website to remember your actions and preferences (such as login, language, font size, and other display preferences) over a period, so you donâ€™t have to keep re-entering them whenever you come back to the site or browse from one page to another. They play a crucial role in enhancing user experience and delivering personalized content. Without cookies, websites would have no memory of your past activities, making every visit a fresh start, which would be highly inconvenient for many functionalities. For instance, an online shopping cart would not be able to remember the items you added as you navigate between product pages.

How We Use Cookies

At Zingtava, we use cookies for various purposes to provide you with a seamless, efficient, and personalized browsing experience. Our use of cookies falls into several categories, each designed to improve different aspects of our website's functionality and your interaction with it. We utilize both first-party cookies, which are set by our website, and third-party cookies, which are set by external services that we integrate for various functionalities like analytics or advertising. The data collected through cookies helps us understand user behavior, optimize content, and ensure the security and performance of our platform. We are committed to transparency regarding our cookie usage and provide you with options to manage your preferences.

This is particularly important for cookies that contain sensitive information, such as session IDs or authentication tokens, to prevent man-in-the-middle attacks. We implement secure cookies wherever sensitive data is involved to protect your privacy and security.

Purpose: To enhance the security of data transmitted via cookies by ensuring encryption.

10. HTTP-Only Cookies

HTTP-only cookies are a security feature that prevents client-side scripts (like JavaScript) from accessing the cookie. This makes it much harder for malicious scripts (e.g., cross-site scripting or XSS attacks) to steal sensitive session cookies. When a cookie is set with the "HttpOnly" flag, it means that the cookie can only be sent to the server as part of an HTTP request and cannot be accessed by JavaScript running in the browser. This significantly reduces the risk of session hijacking, where an attacker could steal a user's session cookie and impersonate them. We utilize HTTP-only cookies for critical authentication and session management purposes.

Purpose: To prevent cross-site scripting (XSS) attacks and enhance the security of sensitive cookies.

Examples: Session IDs, authentication tokens.

Impact of Disabling: Not directly controllable by users; their absence would increase security vulnerabilities.

Data Collected: Same as other cookie types, but with enhanced protection against client-side access.

First-party/Third-party: Can be both first-party or third-party.

Legal Basis: Legitimate interest and security best practices.

User Control: Managed by the website's security configuration, not directly by user consent.

Transparency: We emphasize our security measures, including the use of HTTP-only cookies.

11. SameSite Cookies

SameSite is a cookie attribute that helps mitigate the risk of cross-site request forgery (CSRF) attacks and information leakage. It works by controlling when cookies are sent with cross-site requests. There are three main values: 'Strict', 'Lax', and 'None'. 'Strict' means the cookie is only sent with requests originating from the same site. 'Lax' means the cookie is sent with same-site requests and top-level navigations from other sites (e.g., clicking a link). 'None' means the cookie is sent with all requests, including cross-site requests, but requires the 'Secure' attribute. We implement SameSite attributes to enhance the security and privacy of our users by preventing unintended cookie sharing across sites.

Purpose: To prevent CSRF attacks and control cross-site cookie transmission.

Examples: Session cookies, authentication cookies.

Impact of Disabling: Not directly controllable by users; their absence would increase security vulnerabilities.

Data Collected: Does not collect data itself; it's a security attribute for existing cookies.

Duration: Varies depending on the cookie's primary type.

First-party/Third-party: Applicable to both first-party and third-party cookies.

Legal Basis: Legitimate interest and security best practices.

User Control: Managed by the website's security configuration, not directly by user consent.

Transparency: We highlight our use of modern security attributes like SameSite.
